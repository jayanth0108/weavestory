The Journey of a Thread

1. Cotton Picking

Kala Cotton is hand-picked from rain-fed fields in the arid Rann of Kutch during November–December.

2. Hand Spinning (Charkha)

Raw cotton is cleaned and spun into fine thread using a traditional charkha wheel — entirely by hand.

3. Natural Dyeing

Threads are dyed using plant-based pigments: indigo, turmeric, and iron mordants from local sources.

4. Warping the Loom

Dyed warp threads are carefully wound and mounted onto the pit loom — a process taking 2–3 hours alone.

5. Weaving

The artisan weaves row by row — each stole takes 12–18 hours of continuous weaving to complete.

6. Finishing

The finished fabric is sun-dried, checked for quality, and hand-frilled or hemmed before it reaches you.
